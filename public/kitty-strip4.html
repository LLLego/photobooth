# HELLO KITTY — Taba San Photo Booth Strip I leaned into a **soft pastel craft-paper aesthetic** — a pink kraft-tape strip holding a film photobooth frame against a dreamy candy-pink haze. The composition breaks the "centered title + symmetric grid" cliché by pinning a rotated film strip against the right edge with masking tape on the corners, the way you'd actually pin a photo on a wall. Theatrical decisions worth flagging: **JetBrains Mono decoding reveal on the title** (letter-spacing collapses, blur burns off — like the camera's autofocus catching Kitty's face); **frame drifts -2° then +3° then settles -1°** like it's still settling on the wall; **two sparkle layers** (CSS `★` on the corners + 80 JS-spawned `.sparkle` particles floating up with random rotation/delay/duration); **hearts & sparkles floating through the haze**. All hover- and IntersectionObserver-paused so off-screen it idles at zero CPU. Frame is **560px tall** — TikTok portrait crops at 9:16 still see the full Kitty face and all four panels.
