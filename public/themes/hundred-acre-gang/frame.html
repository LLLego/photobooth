I leaned into a vintage storybook photobooth read here — warm honey-gold rather than the candy-bright Pooh palette, with a sun-gold outer rule and cream deckled paper showing through the borders between corner medallions. Each character is built from layered `border-radius` shapes (tummy circle, ear darts, eye buttons, tiny paw hands) so they feel hand-cut rather than clip-art. The center sits empty with `background: transparent` so you can drop a photo straight in. The corners are framed by tiny oak-leaf flourishes and double-stacked circles — like sticker seals — with the four names (`WINNIE-THE-POOH`, `EEYORE`, `PIGLET`, `TIGGER`) spaced around the frame in storybook italics. The headline `A PHOTO FROM THE HUNDRED ACRE WOOD` anchors the top with a small bee buzzing near it.
