A few decisions worth flagging up-front: - **Palette is anchored on the grey you gave (#A0A0B0)** but I added two cooler siblings — a slate roof `#7C8492` for depth and a pale cloud `#C8CCD2` for the inner mat. Pure flat #A0A0B0 across the whole frame reads like concrete; the two-step value shift lets it feel like flocked paper instead. - **The donkey is full CSS** (about 35 stacked `div`s with `border-radius` blobs — head, two ears with pink inner cups, nose patch, nostrils, eyes with drooping eyebrows, sewn-on button eyes, a single falling tear, mane tufts, body, legs, one slightly-splayed hoof, and a tacked-on tail). His head tilts ~8° down-and-right because Eeyore's whole posture is gravitational. The pink bow sits where the tail attaches, the way it does in the originals. - **The frame stack**: outer matted card → photo window with an inset box-shadow (so it reads as paper laid on paper, not a flat fill) → dashed inner cut line for the physical "tear here" gesture. Photo area is empty with a centred camera glyph, kept faint so whatever photo replaces it can dominate. - **"EEYORE" in small-caps with 8px letter-spacing and "✦" diamonds either side** — the diamonds do the work that an icon font would normally do, without pulling in an external asset. Subtitle below in italic for the signature line. - **Motion is restrained to one loop**: the tear drop falls every 6 seconds and the photo flash blinks at the opposite phase.
