Below is a single-file photobooth frame. I leaned into the Tigger theme by pairing the **Sandbox / Marcellus + Crimson Pro** display pair (Tigger originated in 1920s storybooks, so a letterforms-forward, slightly storybook typography fits better than a generic balloon font) and used **#F4A460** as the orange spine across border, stripes, corner stars, footer and matting. The 1200×1600 frame is built as `aspect-ratio: 3/4` with a max-width so it stays true-to-spec on desktop and scales down on mobile — the saved screenshot at full viewport width will land exactly at 1200×1600. The tiger itself is pure CSS art (~30 nested divs): a rounded body, a cream-tan belly, a head with cream muzzle, two cheeks, pink nose, whiskers, ear interiors, four legs with cream paws, a long S-curved tail with stripe segments, plus the trademark bouncy expression (upward smile + tongue). Stripes use the deepest `#8B4513` so they read at any zoom. Tigger Photo Booth · 1200 × 1600
