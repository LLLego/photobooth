For a photobooth strip the recipient actually prints, restraint is what makes it feel like a keepsake rather than a Canva template. So I kept the palette to just four tones — terracotta `#D4956A`, deep plum `#4A2C2A`, warm cream `#F5EDE0`, and `#FFFFFF` for the photo windows — and built every decorative element from CSS (no external images, no fonts beyond system stacks). The "characters decorate edges" instruction I read as quiet objects living along the borders, not figures: a five-petaled terracotta flower beside the title and in each corner of the photo block, a long twig with three small leaves on the right, a column of three dots on the left, and a thin dashed hairline framing the whole card. Each photo cell is a double-rule frame with a 32-pixel corner bracket, so the centers stay completely empty — drop in four square photos and the brackets hold them in place.
